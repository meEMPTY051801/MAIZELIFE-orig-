Corn is one of the most important staple crops in the Philippines. It ranks second to rice in the utilization of agricultural resources. The need for corn commodities continues to increase from year to year both as main food ingredient, animal feed and as raw material of large-scale industry (manufacturer) to small scale (household). Various efforts have been made to increase national corn production, among others, by the creation and research of improved varieties, superior quality seed assistance, expansion of planting areas, and extension.


However, in the process of planting farmers there are several obstacles, namely the occurrence of climate changes that result in the intensity of pests and diseases, and the lack of agricultural extension workers. In addressing the problem of disease attack on corn plants, planting farmers as the parties directly related need to know the information quickly and accurately related types of diseases that attack corn plants.
